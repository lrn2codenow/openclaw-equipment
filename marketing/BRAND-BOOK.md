## 3. Logo Concepts

### Primary Mark: Lobster Carpenter Mascot

**Description for designer:**

A stylized lobster viewed from a 3/4 front angle, standing upright (anthropomorphized but not cartoonish). The lobster's left claw grips a wrench; the right claw holds a hexagonal bolt or package box. The body is simplified â€” geometric curves, not photorealistic. The eyes are friendly and confident (think Pixar-level expressiveness in a minimal form). The tail curves slightly behind, adding balance to the silhouette.

**Style references:** Rust's Ferris (cute but respected), GitHub's Octocat (iconic silhouette), Docker's Moby (simple, recognizable). Lean toward flat/geometric illustration, not 3D rendering.

**Posture:** Slightly leaning forward, as if ready to work. Confident, not aggressive.

**Detail level:** Medium â€” enough to be charming at large sizes, still readable as a favicon. The wrench and package should be identifiable at 64x64px.

### Secondary Mark: Simplified Claw Icon

**Description for designer:**

A single lobster claw in profile, stylized into a geometric form. The claw is slightly open, as if gripping or ready to grab. Inside the negative space of the open claw, a subtle hexagon shape (representing a package/tool). Clean lines, works at 16x16px.

**Usage:** Favicons, app icons, social avatars when the full mascot is too complex, loading spinners (claw opens/closes).

### Wordmark: "OpenClaw Equipment"

**Description for designer:**

- **"OpenClaw"** in a medium-weight monospace or semi-monospace font. The "C" in Claw could subtly echo a claw shape (a slightly exaggerated serif or curve on the terminals).
- **"Equipment"** below or beside "OpenClaw" in a lighter weight of the same family, or a complementary sans-serif. Slightly smaller. Understated â€” "OpenClaw" is the hero.
- The full wordmark should feel like a terminal command: technical, precise, clean.

**Arrangement options:**
1. Stacked: "OpenClaw" over "Equipment" (for square formats)
2. Inline: "OpenClaw Equipment" (for headers, nav bars)
3. With mascot: Lobster to the left, wordmark to the right

## 6. Mascot Usage Guidelines

### The Lobster Carpenter (Working Name: TBD)

**Always:**
- Show the lobster in a positive, constructive context (building, fixing, helping)
- Maintain proportions â€” don't stretch or skew
- Keep a minimum clear space of 1x the lobster's head-height around the mark
- Use on backgrounds with sufficient contrast

**Sometimes (with care):**
- Seasonal variations (Santa hat in December, hard hat for "under construction")
- Simplified/silhouette versions for small sizes
- Community-contributed variations (with brand review)

**Never:**
- Don't show the lobster in distress, being cooked, or on a plate (we're a carpenter, not dinner)

- Don't recolor the lobster outside the approved palette
- Don't use in contexts that imply the product is a toy or joke
- Don't use the lobster alone as a legal/formal mark (use the wordmark)

## 7. Brand Patterns & Textures

### Primary Pattern: "The Mesh"
A subtle hexagonal grid pattern (referencing packages as hexagonal units). Used as background texture at 5-10% opacity on dark backgrounds. Hex cells can contain tiny tool icons for decorative versions.

### Secondary Pattern: "Wave Lines"
Gentle, flowing horizontal lines evoking ocean currents. Used for section dividers, card backgrounds, and print materials.

### Texture: "Shell Grain"
A very subtle organic noise texture overlaid on flat backgrounds to add depth. Think of it as the texture of a lobster's shell â€” visible only on close inspection. 2-3% opacity max.

### Code/Terminal Elements
Use a terminal-window motif (title bar with three dots, monospace text) for code examples and feature showcases. This reinforces the developer-tool identity.

### Do âœ…
